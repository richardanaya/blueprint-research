Vitamin C

C6H8O6

Blueprint dosage: 250mg (278% DV)

Description

A water-soluble vitamin and powerful antioxidant essential for immune function, collagen synthesis, and cellular protection against oxidative stress.

Mechanism

Acts as an electron donor to neutralize free radicals, supports collagen formation by hydroxylating proline and lysine residues, and enhances immune cell function

Key Benefits

Immune supportAntioxidant protectionCollagen synthesis

Products

Longevity Mix

Research Evidence

Immune support

8/10 High Impact High Confidence

Vitamin C supports immune defense by enhancing various cellular functions of both innate and adaptive immune systems. It accumulates in neutrophils and enhances chemotaxis, phagocytosis, and microbial killing. Meta-analyses show it may reduce the duration of common cold symptoms by 8-14% in adults.

Clinical dosage: 200-1000mg daily
References: [1] [2] [3]

Antioxidant protection

9/10 High Impact High Confidence

As a potent water-soluble antioxidant, vitamin C donates electrons to neutralize free radicals and reactive oxygen species. It regenerates vitamin E from its oxidized form and protects lipids, proteins, and DNA from oxidative damage.

Clinical dosage: 100-1000mg daily
References: [1] [2]

Collagen synthesis

10/10 High Impact High Confidence

Vitamin C is essential for collagen synthesis as a cofactor for prolyl and lysyl hydroxylases, enzymes required for stabilizing the collagen triple helix. Without adequate vitamin C, collagen synthesis is impaired, affecting skin, blood vessels, bones, and wound healing.

Clinical dosage: Minimum 10mg/day to prevent scurvy; optimal 100-200mg
References: [1]

Safety Information

Side Effects High doses may cause digestive upset, May increase kidney stone risk in susceptible individuals
Contraindications Hemochromatosis (iron overload), History of kidney stones
Max Safe Dosage 2000mg/day
